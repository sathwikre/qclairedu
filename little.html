Ratio Analysis: f(n) / g(n)

What Little-o Means

f(n) = o(g(n))

Means:

f(n)/g(n) → 0 as n → ∞

f(n) becomes insignificant compared to g(n).

Common Mistake

Students think:

“smaller” = little-o

Wrong.

The ratio MUST approach 0.

Example:

n = o(n²)

but

n² ≠ o(n²)
