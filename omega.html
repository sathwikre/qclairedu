Big-Ω (Omega) Complexity Visualizer
Ratio Analysis: f(n) / g(n)

What Big-Ω Means

f(n) = Ω(g(n))

Means:

f(n) grows at least as fast as g(n)

Ratio:

f(n)/g(n) does NOT go to 0

Usually stays constant or increases.

Most Common Confusion

Students memorize:

Ω = lower bound

But don't understand it visually.

Big-Ω means:

f(n) never becomes insignificant compared to g(n).
